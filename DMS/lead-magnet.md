## Lead Magnet Concepts

### Concept 1: The Route Planning Audit Checklist
- **The concept:** A self-assessment checklist that helps operators evaluate their current routing system and identify what's costing them time and money
- **Format:** PDF checklist (1-2 pages)
- **The hook:** "The 10-Point Route Audit: Find Out What's Costing You Hours Every Week"
- **The bridge:** Checklist reveals gaps and inefficiencies that RouteFlo directly solves. Each "no" answer maps to a RouteFlo feature. Natural next step: "See how RouteFlo handles this."
- **Implementation:** Low difficulty. Write the checklist, simple design, one landing page.

### Concept 2: The Driver Efficiency Calculator
- **The concept:** Simple calculator where they input number of drivers, stops per day, and time spent routing to see how many hours they're losing and what that costs
- **Format:** Interactive calculator (spreadsheet or simple web tool)
- **The hook:** "How Much Is Manual Routing Costing You? Calculate Your Hidden Costs"
- **The bridge:** Calculator shows the problem in dollars and hours. Output includes "RouteFlo typically saves X hours per week for operations like yours." Clear ROI comparison.
- **Implementation:** Medium difficulty. Needs spreadsheet or simple calculator tool. Could start with Google Sheets version.

### Concept 3: The Route Day Playbook
- **The concept:** A step-by-step guide to running a more efficient route day - from morning planning to end-of-day wrap-up - that works whether they use software or not
- **Format:** PDF guide (5-7 pages)
- **The hook:** "The Route Day Playbook: How Top Mat Delivery Operations Run Their Days"
- **The bridge:** Shows best practices that are easier to implement with software. Positions RouteFlo as the tool that makes this playbook effortless. "Everything in this playbook? RouteFlo does it automatically."
- **Implementation:** Low difficulty. Content + design. Establishes authority and expertise.

### Concept 4: The Customer Route Template
- **The concept:** A ready-to-use spreadsheet template for tracking customers, service days, and routes - better than whatever they're currently using
- **Format:** Spreadsheet template (Google Sheets or Excel)
- **The hook:** "The Free Customer Route Tracker (Better Than Your Current Spreadsheet)"
- **The bridge:** They'll use the template and immediately hit its limitations. "Outgrowing the template? That's exactly why we built RouteFlo." Template includes a "When to switch to software" section.
- **Implementation:** Low difficulty. Build the spreadsheet, add instructions. Genuinely useful even without conversion.

### Concept 5: The Driver Onboarding Checklist
- **The concept:** A checklist for onboarding new drivers quickly - covering routes, customers, procedures
- **Format:** PDF checklist (2-3 pages)
- **The hook:** "New Driver? The 15-Point Checklist to Get Them Route-Ready in One Day"
- **The bridge:** Highlights the chaos of onboarding without a system. "With RouteFlo, new drivers just log in and see their route." Shows the pain of manual onboarding.
- **Implementation:** Low difficulty. Quick to create, evergreen content.

## Recommended Starting Point

**Primary: Concept 1 (Route Planning Audit Checklist)**

Why:
- Lowest implementation effort
- Directly maps problems to RouteFlo features
- Self-qualifying: operators who score poorly are better leads
- Can be created and launched in a day
- Works as standalone value even if they don't convert

**Secondary: Concept 2 (Driver Efficiency Calculator)**

Why:
- ROI calculators convert extremely well for SaaS
- Shows the problem in dollars (speaks to decision-makers)
- Personalized output creates stronger connection
- Higher effort but higher conversion potential

**Sequence suggestion:** Launch Checklist first (quick win), build Calculator next (higher impact).
